## The Regulatory Question

**Does CVS's brokerage license cover fund certificate distribution through MoMo as digital front-end?**

Under Vietnam's Securities Law 2019 (amended 2024) and Circular 98/2020/TT-BTC:
- Chung chi quy (fund certificates) = a type of security under Article 4 Clause 1
- Securities brokerage covers all securities

### Three Scenarios

| Scenario | Probability | Description | Timeline |
|----------|-------------|-------------|----------|
| A — Best case | 85%+ | CVS brokerage license covers fund distribution. Existing stock trading integration = precedent. | 0 months (already covered) |
| B — Moderate | 10% | CVS needs administrative registration as fund distribution agent. Filing, not approval. | 2-4 weeks |
| C — Worst case | ~5% | M_Service needs own distribution agent certificate under tightened Circular 98. | 6-9 months |

**President Thuan's ruling:** Scenario C is effectively OFF THE TABLE. Securities Law 2019 Art. 4(1) confirms fund certificates = securities, brokerage license covers distribution.

## Key Precedents

1. **CVS already runs stock trading through MoMo app** (since May 2024) — same model (CVS backend, MoMo frontend), fund distribution is simpler
2. **VNSC/Finhay distributes VCBF fund certificates** on its platform — if their license scope mirrors CVS, confirms our pathway
3. **MoMo-DCVFM partnership exists since June 2022** — fund certificates may already be available in MoMo app, meaning regulatory pathway already proven

## Strategic Value of June 2025 Amendment

The addition of **proprietary trading (tu doanh chung khoan)** means CVS can hold securities positions on its own book. This matters for:
- Phase 3 equity products
- Market-making capability
- Portfolio hedging
