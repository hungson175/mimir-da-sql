They bought investing capability for their super-app. MoMo already has the building blocks: $490M TTT AUM, 2.88M funded users, 935K self-identified investors, live fund distribution (Dragon Capital, VCBF, IPAAM), and Moni AI.

We have an estimated 18-month window before Grab deploys Stash in Vietnam.

## CRITICAL CORRECTION

The original war plan projected Y1 $2.5M / Y2 $15M / Y3 $45M revenue. **CFO found these overstated by ~50x.** The revenue formula conflated ARPU with distribution fees.

**Corrected (board-approved):**
- Y1: $50-200K (this is a strategic loss year)
- Y2: $1.4M (distribution fees + Tui+ subscription bundle)
- Y3: $4.5M (subscription at scale)

The $45M number must be removed from any deck before it reaches your desk. It is indefensible.

## KEY DECISIONS MADE

1. **Subscription model is mandatory** — distribution fees alone ($0.20-0.34/user/year) cannot sustain the product. Revenue vehicle: Tui+ bundle at VND 19K/mo ($0.75).
2. **TTT Guardrails** (not wall) — protect $11.5M/yr TTT spread. Default fund purchases from wallet, not TTT. Track migration weekly. Review trigger at 10% AUM migration.
3. **Target Segments 2+4 first** — 935K self-ID'd investors + 1.2M power users. NOT the 9.9M micro-savers (86% hold <$12).
4. **Capital preservation products only Phase 1** — money market + short-term bond. No equity funds.
5. **Month-6 kill gate** — 100K funded users + 15% Tui+ bundle uptake + NPS>30. Miss 2 of 3 = pause. Max loss if killed: ~$800K.
6. **Budget: $1.5-2M** (not $500K-1M as in original deck — that only covered the 90-day launch).
