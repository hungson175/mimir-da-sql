## Slide 7: The GrabInvest Autopsy

GrabInvest launched in 2020 and shut down in September 2023. Understanding why they failed is the key to our success.

### Why GrabInvest Failed

| Severity | Reason |
|----------|--------|
| **FATAL** | **Weak yields (1.18-2.5%)** -- Did not beat bank deposits. No reason to switch. |
| **FATAL** | **No behavioral hook** -- Generic money market fund. No round-ups, no rewards, no goals. |
| **MAJOR** | **Wrong market** -- Singapore's affluent population doesn't need micro-investing. |
| **MAJOR** | **Context mismatch** -- Users came for transport, not investing. Adjacent intent too weak. |

> Sources: Kapronasia 2023, DealStreetAsia 2023, Vulcan Post 2023 [Tier2/Stale but events are historical]

### How MoMo Avoids Every Trap

| GrabInvest Problem | MoMo Answer |
|--------------------|-------------|
| Weak yields | **Competitive yields** -- TTT already pays ~4%. Partner funds target 6-9%. Vietnam bank deposits: 4-6%. |
| No behavioral hook | **Behavioral engine** -- Round-ups from 375M txn/mo. Goal-based savings. Moni AI coaching. Gamification. |
| Wrong market | **Right market** -- Vietnam: young (61% TTT users <28), low bank deposits, high smartphone adoption. |
| Context mismatch | **Native context** -- Users come for payments + savings. Finance IS the use case. Not a bolt-on. |

### 5 Success Factors for Embedded Investing

| Factor | MoMo Status |
|--------|-------------|
| 1. Compelling yield (must beat bank deposits) | YES |
| 2. Zero-friction onboarding (leverage existing eKYC) | YES |
| 3. Behavioral mechanism (round-ups, rewards, goals) | **BUILD THIS** |
| 4. Distribution + trust (large user base, brand equity) | YES |
| 5. Product-market fit (match local financial psychology) | **VALIDATE** |

## Slide 9: Revenue Engine

Revenue comes from fund managers (distribution fees), not users. Zero-cost for users = zero friction. This is the Ant Fortune model adapted for Vietnam.

### Fee Architecture

| Revenue Stream | Source | Rate |
|----------------|--------|------|
| **Distribution fee** | Fund managers (B2B) | 0.15-0.40% of AUM p.a. |
| **TTT spread (existing)** | Interest margin | ~2% net spread on $490M |
| **Premium subscription** | Users (Phase 2+) | VND 25-50K/mo ($1-2) |
| **Gold/commodity spread** | Buy/sell spread | 0.5-1.5% per transaction |

## Slide 12: Risk Matrix & Mitigations

### Risk Assessment

| Level | Risk | Description | Mitigation | Owner |
|-------|------|-------------|------------|-------|
| **HIGH** | Investment losses damage brand | If users lose money on MoMo-distributed funds, brand trust could be damaged irreparably. | **Start with capital preservation products only** (money market, short-term bond). No equity funds in Phase 1. Clear disclaimers. Education-first UX. | CPO + Legal |
| **HIGH** | Yield compression | Vietnam's interest rates may decline. If fund yields fall below bank deposits (4-6%), value proposition collapses (see: Yu'e Bao from 7% to 1.27%). | **Don't compete on yield alone.** Build behavioral hooks (round-ups, goals, gamification) that retain users even at lower yields. Diversify beyond MMFs. | CMO + CFO |
| **MED** | Regulatory change | SSC could tighten distribution agent rules or impose new compliance burdens. | **Maintain close SSC relationship.** Consider applying for securities company license (VND 25B, ~$1M). Monitor Resolution 222 IFC implementation. | Legal + CPO |
| **MED** | Grab enters faster than expected | If Grab accelerates Stash Vietnam deployment, our 18-month window shrinks. | **Speed is the mitigation.** Phase 1 uses existing infrastructure = launch in 60-90 days. By the time Grab enters, we should have 500K+ funded users. | CEO + CMO |
| **LOW** | Finhay/Tikop competitive response | Finhay (3M users) or Tikop could accelerate marketing or cut pricing. | **Our scale advantage is decisive.** Finhay has 300K funded users vs our 2.88M TTT funded. They cannot match our distribution. | CMO |
