Risk Assessment 12 / 13

Risk Matrix & Mitigations

Honest assessment of what could go wrong, and specific mitigations for each risk.

Level Risk Description Mitigation Owner
HIGH Investment losses damage brand If users lose money on funds distributed by MoMo, brand trust (MoMo's #1 asset) could be damaged irreparably. Start with capital preservation products only (money market, short-term bond). No equity funds in Phase 1. Clear disclaimers. Education-first UX. CPO + Legal
HIGH Yield compression Vietnam's interest rates may decline (following global trend). If fund yields fall below bank deposits (4-6%), value proposition collapses (see: Yu'e Bao from 7% to 1.27%). Don't compete on yield alone. Build behavioral hooks (round-ups, goals, gamification) that retain users even at lower yields. Diversify beyond MMFs. CMO + CFO
MED Regulatory change SSC could tighten distribution agent rules, require additional licensing, or impose new compliance burdens. Maintain close SSC relationship. Consider applying for securities company license as insurance (VND 25B capital, ~$1M). Monitor Resolution 222 IFC implementation. Legal + CPO
MED Grab enters faster than expected If Grab accelerates Stash Vietnam deployment (e.g., partnering with local fund manager), our 18-month window shrinks. Speed is the mitigation. Phase 1 uses existing infrastructure = launch in 60-90 days. By the time Grab enters, we should have 500K+ funded users. CEO + CMO
LOW Finhay/Tikop competitive response Finhay (3M users, securities license) or Tikop could accelerate marketing spend or cut pricing. Our scale advantage is decisive. Finhay has 300K funded users vs our 2.88M TTT funded. They cannot match our distribution at any price point. CMO

Confirmation bias We want this to work, so we emphasize our 30M users. Counter: 86% hold <$12 in TTT. They may not want to invest.
Recency bias Grab's Stash deal creates urgency. But their SEA timeline is "longer-term." We have time to be methodical.
