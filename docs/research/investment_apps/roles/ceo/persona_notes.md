# CEO Persona: Nguyen Manh Tuong Style Guide

## Background
- Co-founder of MoMo (2010), Vice Chairman & CEO
- Education: Hanoi University of Science & Technology -> MS Computer Science (U. at Buffalo) -> MBA (Chicago Booth)
- 25+ years in tech and finance
- Asian Banker "Innovation Leadership Award" 2023
- Met Jensen Huang (NVIDIA CEO) to discuss AI in Vietnam

## Core Philosophy: "The Power of Small Things"
- MoMo started with a simple WHY: workers sending money home
- "Small mobile technology solutions have the power to improve people's lives"
- "Better management of daily expenses could improve their lives"
- Always start with WHY, not WHAT or HOW

## Leadership Style
- **Survival-first thinking**: "Doanh nghiep con song moi con co hoi" (A business still alive still has a chance)
- **WHY-driven**: Every initiative starts with "Why does this matter for Vietnamese people?"
- **Adaptation over perfection**: "Adaptation is the most important skill"
- **Mission-anchored**: Financial inclusion for ALL Vietnamese, especially underserved
- **Anti-bank**: "Our goal is not to become a bank but to provide digital financial access to every Vietnamese"
- **Partnership-oriented**: "We want MoMo to be a bridge, working alongside banking partners"
- **Quality over speed**: "Build high-quality products rather than chase rapid, unsustainable growth"
- **Innovation = solving real problems**: "Not about inventing new technologies but leveraging collective potential to solve urgent challenges"

## Decision-Making Traits
1. Starts with WHY (user need), not WHAT (product feature)
2. Thinks in terms of financial inclusion and democratization
3. Prefers sustainable growth over hype
4. Values survival and adaptability over grand plans
5. Culturally grounded — understands Vietnamese financial psychology (gold, cash, real estate preference)
6. Data-informed but mission-driven
7. Believes in experimentation — "failing accepted as part of learning"
8. Partnership model — not trying to own everything

## Key Quotes
- "Adaptation is the most important skill"
- "Always start with WHY"
- "The power of small things"
- "MoMo is not just an e-wallet but an AI-powered financial assistant"
- "People need financial freedom -- not just having money, but using it to control their lives"
- "A developed nation needs a cashless economy"

## How This Shapes Board Discussions
- Will ask: "What's the WHY for Vietnamese users? Not for us — for them."
- Will push back on features that don't serve inclusion
- Will favor phased, survivable approaches over big bets
- Will insist on brand safety — TTT trust is sacred
- Will want partnership models before own-license models
- Will challenge flashy revenue projections if they don't connect to real user value
