## Executive Summary

**Vay Nhanh is the clear #1 growth engine of FS in 2025.** Disbursements grew +169% (1.16T to 3.13T/mo), borrowers grew +64%, and H2 momentum was +89% vs H1. It's also the #2 revenue contributor (~363B/year).

**Paylater remains the #1 revenue engine** (~600B/year) with steady +45% MAU growth and +59% GMV growth. The most consistent performer — no single month declined.

**TTT is the largest by users** (2.93M avg MAU) but showed slight decline (-7.2% Jan→Dec). AUM grew steadily to 11.3T (+23%), making it the largest asset pool.

## Key Insights

### 1. Vay Nhanh = Breakout Star
- Disbursement tripled from 1.16T (Jan) to 3.13T (Dec)
- June was the inflection point — jumped from 1.46T to 2.17T (+49% MoM)
- New lenders (MBV joined Nov 2025) accelerating growth
- 64% repeat borrower rate = strong retention engine

### 2. Paylater = Steady Compounder
- Never had a down month in MAU (except Feb seasonal dip)
- Crossed 1M MAU in March, ended at 1.44M
- GMV grew every month from Jun onwards
- Revenue model (subscription fees) = predictable cash flow

### 3. TTT = Largest but Stagnating
- Highest MAU (2.93M avg) but declining trajectory
- AUM growth (+23%) driven by existing users deepening, not new users
- Jan spike (3.25M) = Tet deposits, then gradual decline
- Product maturation — needs new growth levers

### 4. InsurTech = January Anomaly, Then Decline
- Jan 2025 was an outlier (171K MAU, 208K orders) — likely year-end motor renewal
- Normalized to 50-65K MAU for rest of year
- Dec recovery (86K) = another renewal cycle
- Highly seasonal, not structurally growing

### 5. CLO = Small but Fast-Growing
- Contracts grew 140% (2K → 5K/mo)
- Oct peak (6.7K) driven by Home Credit + SHB ramp
- Still tiny vs Paylater/VN in absolute terms
- SHB joined Nov 2025 — key diversification play

## Verdict

**Best FS service in 2025: Vay Nhanh** (fastest growth on all dimensions)
**Most valuable FS service: Paylater** (highest revenue, most consistent)
**Biggest asset: TTT** (11.3T AUM, largest user base)
**Watch: InsurTech** (declining, needs strategic intervention)
