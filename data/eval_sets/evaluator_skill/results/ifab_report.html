Important Caveat

The Unfair Advantage Factor

Before drawing conclusions, consider the confounding variable: the underlying LLM.

This comparison is not fully controlled

The two systems use different underlying LLMs. This means the performance gap reflects both the architecture differences (domain knowledge, prompting, metadata quality) AND the raw LLM capability gap. We cannot isolate which factor contributes more without a controlled rerun.

What This Means

Some of Claude DA's advantage—particularly in complex multi-step queries and business rule inference—may stem from GPT-4o's superior reasoning ability rather than the domain knowledge architecture. The gap on Simple queries (0.78 vs 0.52) may be more indicative of the architecture difference, while the Complex gap could be inflated by the LLM difference.

Recommendation

Run a controlled evaluation where both systems use the same LLM backend (e.g., both on GPT-4o or both on Gemini Flash). This isolates the contribution of domain knowledge, prompting strategy, and metadata quality—which are the factors we can actually improve.

Until rerun: treat the 1.96× gap as an upper bound, not a precise measurement.
However—Infrastructure Failures Are Real

25 of Mimir's 62 questions (40%) failed due to infrastructure issues unrelated to the LLM: 11 backend timeouts, 8 schema mismatches, 5 date bugs (Feb 29 in non-leap year), 1 column not found. Even controlling for LLM, these failures represent systemic gaps in Mimir's metadata and backend reliability that need to be fixed independently.
